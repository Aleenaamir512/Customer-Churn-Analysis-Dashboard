# Replace 1 with 'Yes' and 0 with 'No'
df['Churn'] = df['Churn'].replace({1: 'Yes', 0: 'No'})

# 1. Count of churned vs not churned
churn_counts = df['Churn'].value_counts()

# 2. Labels and values
labels = churn_counts.index
values = churn_counts.values

# 3. Pie chart
plt.figure(figsize=(6, 6))
plt.pie(values, labels=labels, autopct='%1.1f%%', startangle=90, 
        wedgeprops={'edgecolor': 'white'})

plt.title("Customer Churn Distribution")
plt.tight_layout()
plt.show()
This distribution shows how much people churn the subscription and how much people not churn. Yes means 47.4% people churn and No means 52.6% people not churn Conclusion: 52.6% people not churn the subscription. Most people using and churning are women. People churning are mostly from monthly subsciption. Yearly and Quarterly subsciptions are not easily churned.
